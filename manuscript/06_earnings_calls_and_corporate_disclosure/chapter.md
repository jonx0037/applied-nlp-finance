# Chapter 6 — Earnings Calls and Corporate Disclosure

This chapter systematically analyzes scheduled, high-signal financial language events. It covers the structure of earnings calls, linguistic features predictive of post-earnings drift, and NLP approaches to 10-K/10-Q risk factor analysis. The chapter establishes event study methodology for text-based signals.

## Structure of Earnings Calls

Decompose earnings calls into prepared remarks and Q&A segments, explaining why each carries distinct signal characteristics. The prepared remarks reflect carefully crafted corporate messaging, while Q&A captures spontaneous executive responses to analyst scrutiny.

## Linguistic Features Predictive of Post-Earnings Drift

Identify uncertainty language, hedging, specificity, and other linguistic markers that predict stock price movement after earnings events. Research shows that linguistic complexity and evasiveness in earnings calls carry incremental predictive power beyond the numeric earnings surprise itself.

## Tone Analysis: CEO vs. CFO Language

Compare language patterns between executives and analyze how analyst question tone provides additional signal. CEOs tend toward optimistic framing while CFOs are more measured, and the gap between their tones can itself be informative.

## Risk Factor Analysis: 10-K and 10-Q

Apply NLP to track year-over-year changes in risk factor language as a signal for emerging corporate risks. Changes in boilerplate risk factor language — additions, deletions, and rewording — often telegraph material developments before they appear in earnings.

## MD&A as a Structured NLP Target

Treat Management Discussion and Analysis sections as a rich, semi-structured text source amenable to systematic analysis. MD&A sections follow predictable topical structure while containing management's forward-looking narrative, making them ideal for systematic extraction.

## Event Study Methodology for Text-Based Signals

Establish rigorous event study frameworks for evaluating whether text-derived features carry genuine predictive power around corporate events. Proper event study design controls for confounders, establishes statistical significance, and distinguishes genuine signal from data mining artifacts.
