# Chapter 1 — Why Text Moves Markets

This chapter makes the conceptual case for financial NLP before a single line of code is written. It establishes why financial markets are narrative-driven systems and how natural language processing has matured enough to systematically extract signal from financial text. The chapter provides the intellectual foundation for everything that follows.

## The Efficient Market Hypothesis and Information

Examine the EMH's assumptions about how information is incorporated into prices, and challenge the implicit assumption that "information" is numeric. The semi-strong form assumes all public information is reflected in prices, but it says nothing about the speed or mechanism by which linguistic information is processed.

## What "Information" Means When It Arrives as Language

Explore the distinction between quantitative data and linguistic data, and why the latter carries unique signal about market participant beliefs and intentions. Language encodes uncertainty, intention, and framing in ways that numeric data cannot.

## Historical Examples: Text Moving Markets

Walk through concrete cases — Fed minutes moving bond markets, earnings call tone predicting stock drift, Twitter sentiment preceding price moves — to ground the theoretical argument in observable market behavior. Each example illustrates a different mechanism by which text creates or reveals information asymmetry.

## Why NLP Has Lagged Price/Volume Quant Strategies

Analyze the technical, data, and institutional barriers that historically prevented systematic text analysis in finance, and explain why those barriers are now falling. The convergence of transformer models, accessible APIs, and cloud compute has fundamentally changed the cost-benefit calculation.

## A Taxonomy of Financial Text Data

Classify financial text sources along two dimensions: structured vs. unstructured and scheduled vs. unscheduled. This taxonomy frames the data acquisition strategy in Chapter 2 and helps readers understand which text sources carry which types of signal.
